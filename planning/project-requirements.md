## 3. Project Overview  
A simple online ordering manager enabling stores to host their menus online, receive orders from customers, and manage these orders through a dedicated business frontend where they can confirm or decline incoming orders.

---

## 4. Problem Statement  
Many small businesses lack a streamlined way to present their menus and accept/manage online orders. This project aims to provide an easy-to-use, two-sided platform: one for customers to browse and submit orders, and another for business owners to review, accept, or reject orders efficiently.

---

## 5. Key Features (User Stories)  
- As a store owner, I want to create and update my menu online so that customers always see current offerings.
- As a customer, I want to browse the menu and place an order online so that I can order conveniently from my device.
- As a store owner, I want to see incoming orders in real-time on a business dashboard so that I can process them quickly.
- As a store owner, I want to confirm or decline orders so that customers are updated on their order status.
- As a customer, I want to receive confirmation or rejection notifications after placing an order so I know the status.
- (Add further user stories as the project progresses.)

---

## 6. Out of Scope  
- Payment processing (no online payments in v1)
- Delivery tracking or logistics
- Multi-store management (single store per account for initial version)
- Advanced analytics or reporting dashboards

---

## 7. Success Criteria  
- Store owners can create, edit, and publish menus online.
- Customers can view menus and place orders without friction.
- Orders submitted by customers appear instantly in the business dashboard.
- Store owners can confirm or decline orders, with customers notified of status changes.
- The interface is clear and easy to use for both store owners and customers.

---

## 8. Timeline / Milestones  
- Week 1: MVP — Core platform foundation, single-store menu CRUD, basic customer order flow, and owner order management.
- Week 2: Enhancements — Add order notifications, improve UI/UX, error handling, minor features, and prepare for deployment.
